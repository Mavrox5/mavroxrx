Performax is a compounded, multi-mechanistic sublingual tablet designed to support erectile response in men, especially those who have not responded optimally to standard single-agent PDE5 inhibitors. It combines three active ingredients (Apomorphine, Tadalafil, and Vardenafil) that work across both central arousal pathways and peripheral vascular mechanisms simultaneously.
Place ½ to 1 full tablet under your tongue and allow it to dissolve completely. Do not chew or swallow whole. Take it 30–60 minutes before sexual activity. New patients should start with half a tablet to assess tolerance. Do not exceed one dose per 48-hour period.
Standard ED medications like Viagra or Cialis use a single PDE5 inhibitor. Performax combines two PDE5 inhibitors (Tadalafil and Vardenafil) with Apomorphine, a dopamine receptor agonist that activates the arousal signal in the brain. This triple-agent approach addresses mechanisms that single-agent treatments miss entirely, making it effective for men who haven't had success elsewhere.
Common side effects may include headache, flushing, nasal congestion, and mild nausea (most associated with the Apomorphine component). These are generally mild and transient. Do not use Performax if you take nitrates for chest pain, have severely low blood pressure, or have been advised against sexual activity due to cardiovascular conditions. Consult your provider if you have any concerns.
